Literature Review

December 2015
  • Literature Review
  • Research Paper
  • Latex


I had to write a literature review
I hadn't written anything in years, I code applications up and sometimes do a bit of design.
It would be a challenge I knew.

The title of the paper is:
The advantages of using JavaScript for full stack development with an emphasis on Node.js

I knew I wanted to do my paper on some bleeding edge technology, the problem being that most research papers are quite old.
I got a membership for the ACM and the IEEE research sites.
The first step was scour these sites for relevant papers.

I decided that I wanted to look into the performance of Node.js since this was a hot topic for debate among my peers.
I found this wasn't enough so the review turned out to be on JS for full stack development.

I printed out endless amounts of papers, read through, highlighted parts in different colours and collated all the data.
I felt like a mad scientist, papers everywhere, writing, rewriting, it was nuts.

I wrote it up in Latex which was great because you could use version control (git) and work collaboratively on the document.
Also the typesetting is amazing.

It's short, my first proper endevour into the world of writing but it was quite a personal achievement

You can view the literature review here: Research Paper

Gesture Controlled Robot

April 2016
  • C
  • Arduino
  • Johnny-Five
  • Electronics
  • Myo-Armband
  • Cortana
  • Node
  • Robots


Robotics:
The branch of technology that deals with the design, construction, operation, and application of robots.

I created an Arduino robot with the Johnny-Five JS framework that is controlled via the Myo armband with gestures.


Personality
The robot is the love child of Bender and Johnny-Five.
Hence his name is Bender-Five (obviously).


Conclusion:
Johnny-Five is great if you only know JavaScript, are scared of electronics or you just hate C and want to get into robotics/electronics.

I think even with the above intentions once you learn Johnny-Five you will quickly move onto C/C++. This is not a bad thing, Johnny-Five is a great stepping stone into the more serious stuff. Kind of like MineCraft for Java programming. I think maybe for kids who know JS it would be really great.

Although I do find the Arduino starter-kit a pretty good C stepping stone!

I found that it was really quick and easy to setup Johnny-Five projects. If you want to quickly actualize your vision then it's great, but it leaves you with a feeling of "What is actually happening here?".
